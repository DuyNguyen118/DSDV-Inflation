CORE
INSIGHT

On paper, Vietnam's inflation looks controlled. But headline stability masks a deeper truth: macroeconomic success has decoupled from household welfare.

While GDP grows, the cost of essentials—food, housing, education—persistently rises. Our data reveals that Vietnam hasn't solved inflation; it has merely redistributed the burden onto those least able to absorb it.

Sectoral Persistence

Food prices inflate almost yearly. Education sees uninterrupted hikes. Transport is the only volatile sector, often masking the steady rise in essentials.

No Relief Years

Read vertically, essential sectors are dominated by red blocks. Even in moderate inflation years (2015-2019), households faced sharp Healthcare spikes, creating hidden pressure.

Structural Vulnerability

Inflation is driven by the persistent rise of daily necessities. Brief relief periods (blue blocks) come only from temporary fuel price drops, not structural improvements.

2021
The Energy Buffer. Global energy prices acted as a shield. The large deflationary flow (-15.82%) neutralized the rising cost of Food, keeping headline CPI artificially low despite supply chain disruptions.
2022
The Macro-Micro Divergence. Energy flipped to become the primary inflation engine (+25.78%). While GDP surged (8.02%), Real Wages fell (-3.15%), meaning national growth was purchased at the direct cost of household purchasing power.
